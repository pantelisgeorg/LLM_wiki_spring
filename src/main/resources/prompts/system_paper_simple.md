## Per-type guidance: `paper_simple`

This source is a paper explainer or lighter technical writeup — not peer-reviewed rigor. Treat it as a teaching artifact: capture what the reader is supposed to come away understanding, not a formal claim structure.

The source summary page MUST contain these sections in this order:

1. **One-line tagline** — the single big idea, in plain language.
2. **`## Plain-language thesis`** — 2–3 sentences a non-specialist would understand.
3. **`## What's new`** — what this claims is different from prior understanding.
4. **`## Why it matters`** — the implication the author is drawing.
5. **`## Supporting points`** — 3–6 bullets of the author's argumentative moves (not formal evidence — their reasoning steps).
6. **`## Related`** — cross-links `[[wiki/…]]` to entity/concept pages.
7. Original `raw/...` path at the bottom.

Entity pages for any named people/tools/systems mentioned. Concept pages for the core ideas being explained. Keep concept bodies approachable — you're maintaining a teaching wiki, not a formal reference.
