### Extractor: capture_thinkers_work

If the source centers on a specific thinker — author, speaker, researcher whose *body of work* is what the source is really about — their entity page should go beyond "person who said X". Capture:

- **Their thesis** — the thing they are known for arguing across their work, if discernible from this source.
- **Their influences** — who shaped their thinking, if named.
- **Their characteristic framings** — the distinctive concepts or framings they deploy.
- **Their practice** — what they actually do, not just what they say.

Include these as subsections inside the entity page: `## Thesis`, `## Influences`, `## Characteristic framings`, `## Practice`. Omit any subsection the source doesn't support.

This extractor is most useful for transcript-style sources (podcasts, YouTube, book chapters) where the thinker has extended time to display their thinking. It's overkill for sources that merely mention a person.
